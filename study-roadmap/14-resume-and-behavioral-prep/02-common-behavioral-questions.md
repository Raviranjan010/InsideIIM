# 10 Common Behavioral Questions

Behavioral questions evaluate how you handle real-world work scenarios, conflicts, failures, and technical growth. Interviewers look for evidence of collaboration, problem-solving, resilience, and accountability.

Here are the top 10 behavioral questions you should prepare for:

---

### Q1. Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a team member or classmate on a technical project. How did you resolve it?
*   **What they are looking for:** Empathy, communication skills, and the ability to separate personal feelings from technical tradeoffs to reach a constructive compromise.

### Q2. Describe a time you made a major mistake or had a significant bug in your code. What did you do?
*   **What they are looking for:** Accountability (not blaming others), immediate troubleshooting communication, and putting safeguards in place to prevent the mistake from happening again.

### Q3. Tell me about a time you had to work with a technology or framework you had never used before. How did you handle it?
*   **What they are looking for:** Your learning strategy, self-reliance, resourcefulness, and how quickly you can acquire enough skills to deliver working code.

### Q4. Tell me about a time you had to deliver a project under a very tight deadline. How did you prioritize tasks?
*   **What they are looking for:** Pragmatism, scope-reduction capabilities (building a Minimum Viable Product), time management, and proactive communication about tradeoffs.

### Q5. Describe a situation where you had to explain a complex technical concept to a non-technical stakeholder or user.
*   **What they are looking for:** Communication adjustment, avoiding gatekeeping jargon, using relatable analogies, and verifying understanding.

### Q6. Tell me about a time when your initial implementation plan failed during development. How did you adapt?
*   **What they are looking for:** Pivot speed, analytical thinking under pressure, and flexibility in adapting to new constraints.

### Q7. Describe a time when you received constructive feedback that you disagreed with. How did you respond?
*   **What they are looking for:** Receptiveness, emotional maturity, active listening, and the ability to evaluate feedback objectively.

### Q8. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond to solve a technical issue or deliver a feature.
*   **What they are looking for:** Ownership mindset, persistence, curiosity, and pride in quality work.

### Q9. Give me an example of a time you had to make a technical decision with incomplete information or data.
*   **What they are looking for:** Risk management, building prototype spikes to test assumptions, and making logical deductions under uncertainty.

### Q10. Tell me about a time you noticed an inefficiency or bug in a codebase that wasn't directly assigned to you. What did you do?
*   **What they are looking for:** Initiative, proactivity, care for the team's shared repository, and clean collaboration.
